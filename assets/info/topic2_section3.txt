The 24/7 nature of digital connectivity has amplified the severity of social harassment. Recent statistics from 2026 reveal that approximately 53.9% of U.S. teens report being cyberbullied, with platforms like YouTube and TikTok seeing the highest prevalence. Short-term impacts on individuals include acute emotional distress, such as sudden anger, shame, and "somatic symptoms" like stomach aches or insomnia. Long-term impacts are devastating, often resulting in chronic depression, social anxiety, and a significantly higher risk of suicidal ideation compared to traditional bullying. Impacts on society include increased school absenteeism, higher dropout rates, and a general erosion of digital safety, forcing communities to invest heavily in specialized mental health support and anti-bullying legislation.
