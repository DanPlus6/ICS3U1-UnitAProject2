Despite digital advancements, excessive paper consumption from printing remains a severe environmental threat.  Globally, over 400 million tons of paper are produced annually, with Americans alone using about 700 pounds of paper per person every year.

In the short term, paper production consumes immense resources, requiring 10,000 to 20,000 gallons of water per ton of paper, while releasing toxic ink and bleaching chemicals into local waterways. Long-term impacts are driven by massive deforestation, as 35% to 40% of all harvested trees are used for paper, systematically destroying critical global carbon sinks.

The impact on human society includes chronic public health issues in communities located near heavily polluting pulp mills. The impact on Earth is compounded at the end of the product's life; paper waste comprises up to 35% of landfill content, where it decomposes and releases methane, a highly potent greenhouse gas.
