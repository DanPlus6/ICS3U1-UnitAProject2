A paperless workplace policy aims to minimize or entirely eliminate the use of physical paper by transitioning to digital documents, electronic signatures, and cloud-based management systems. This approach significantly reduces an organization's environmental footprint. By decreasing the demand for paper, the policy helps combat deforestation and preserves natural ecosystems. It also saves the massive amounts of water and energy traditionally required for paper manufacturing, while reducing the e-waste generated by discarded printers, ink cartridges, and toners. According to research published in the European Journal of Sustainable Development Research (Yousufi, 2023), paper production is a major industrial consumer of wood and a top source of landfill waste. By adopting digital document management, companies can directly lower their carbon footprint, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and minimize solid waste generation.
