Ergonomics standards are workplace policies designed to optimize the physical setup of computer workstations to safely fit the user's body. These standards typically mandate the use of adjustable chairs with proper lumbar support, specific monitor heights and viewing distances (usually 50–70 cm away), flat keyboard placement, and regular movement breaks. This policy directly mitigates negative health impacts—such as musculoskeletal disorders (MSDs) like carpal tunnel syndrome, chronic back pain, and eye strain. It works by preventing awkward, static postures and repetitive joint stress that fatigue muscles and restrict blood flow. According to guidelines from the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), implementing strict ergonomic interventions significantly lowers the frequency of work-related physical pain, protecting the long-term health of computer users.
