Artificial intelligence and computer vision are revolutionizing the recycling industry by significantly improving the efficiency of waste sorting. Traditional recycling facilities often rely on manual sorting, which is slow and can result in large amounts of recyclable material incorrectly ending up in landfills. Computers equipped with machine learning algorithms can rapidly identify different types of plastics, metals, and paper on a fast-moving conveyor belt.

AMP Robotics as example, uses an AI platform combined with robotic arms. These computer-guided robots use optical sensors to recognize patterns, shapes, and textures in the trash. The AI can sort up to 80 recyclable items per minute with 99% accuracy--which is approximately twice the speed of human sorters. By recovering high-purity materials so efficiently, this computer technology directly diverts valuable resources from landfills and pushes them back into the supply chain.
