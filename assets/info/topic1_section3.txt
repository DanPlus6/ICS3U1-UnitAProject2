The constant cycle of manufacturing, upgrading, and discarding computer hardware has created an unprecedented electronic waste crisis.  According to the UN's Global E-waste Monitor, a record 62 million tonnes of e-waste were generated globally in 2022, yet only 22.3% was formally collected and properly recycled.

Short-term impacts feature immediate toxic exposure for millions of informal waste pickers—often women and children—who resort to burning plastics and using acid baths to extract valuable metals. Long-term impacts include the squandering of finite rare-earth metals, which forces continued, destructive mining operations to replace lost materials.

Societally, this impacts human society by causing severe neurological and developmental health crises in developing nations where e-waste is disproportionately dumped. The impact on Earth is devastating, as discarded electronics leach hazardous heavy metals like lead and mercury deep into the soil and groundwater, permanently contaminating ecosystems.
