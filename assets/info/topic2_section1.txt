The rise of computer-based work and entertainment has fostered a global "sedentary epidemic." Recent statistics from the WHO (2025/2026) indicate that 81% of adolescents and 1 in 3 adults do not meet physical activity guidelines due to increased technology use. Short-term impacts on individuals include reduced daily calorie burn, immediate muscle stiffness, and poor circulation. In the long term, this inactivity leads to "sitting disease," characterized by chronic conditions like Type 2 diabetes, obesity, and cardiovascular disease. Impacts on society are profound; the WHO predicts nearly 500 million new cases of preventable noncommunicable diseases by 2030, potentially costing the global economy $27 billion annually in healthcare expenses and lost productivity.
