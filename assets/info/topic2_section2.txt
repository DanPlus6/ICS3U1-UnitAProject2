Digital Eye Strain (DES), or Computer Vision Syndrome, is now a primary ocular health concern. Recent statistics show that up to 75-90% of heavy computer users report symptoms, with the average adult in 2026 spending over 10 hours daily on digital screens. Short-term impacts include "ocular surface dysfunction," which manifests as dry, gritty eyes, blurred vision, and tension headaches. In the long term, individuals may face "accommodative lag" (difficulty refocusing) and accelerated myopia (nearsightedness) progression, especially in children. Impacts on society include a measurable dip in workplace accuracy and productivity, with annual economic losses exceeding $2 billion due to vision-related discomfort and errors.
