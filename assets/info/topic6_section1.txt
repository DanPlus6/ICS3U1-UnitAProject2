Smart agriculture utilizes Internet of Things (IoT) sensors and computer algorithms to monitor environmental conditions in real-time, drastically reducing agricultural water consumption. Instead of watering crops on a fixed schedule, farmers use computers to analyze data on soil moisture, crop health, and weather forecasts. This allows for precision irrigation, delivering water exactly when and where it is needed.

Companies like CropX manufacture IoT soil monitoring systems. These connected sensors transmit subsurface soil data to a central computer system. The AI analyzes the data and automates the irrigation system to apply the precise amount of water required for that specific zone. According to reports by the World Economic Forum, implementing IoT-driven precision agriculture technologies can reduce farm water usage by up to 30%, conserving a critical natural resource while maintaining crop yields.
